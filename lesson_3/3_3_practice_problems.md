The three levels of schema are: conceptual, logical, and physical.

2.

A conceptual schema is a high-level overview of the data-possessing entities
in our business logic and the types of relationship between their data
(one-to-one, one-to-many, many-to-many).

LS answer: a high-level design focused on identifying entities and their
relationships.

3.

A physical schema is a database-specific design incorporating details about the
fields, data types, and constraints of database tables.

LS answer: a low-level database-specific design focused on implementation.
